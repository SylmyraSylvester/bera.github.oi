Taman Awam Bera

Taman Awam Bera (Bera Public Park)

This is a large park located in the town of Bera. The park has a variety of amenities, including a playground, a lake, and a jogging track. It's a great place to relax and enjoy the outdoors.

Bera Lake

Bera Lake

Bera Lake (Malay: Tasik Bera) is a natural freshwater lake located in Bera District, Pahang, Malaysia. It is the largest freshwater swamp in Peninsular Malaysia and a biodiversity hotspot.

Covering approximately 6,150 hectares, Lake Bera is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna, including many endangered species. The lake and its surrounding wetlands are recognized for their ecological importance and are protected under the Ramsar Convention.

Visitors to Lake Bera can enjoy activities such as bird watching, fishing, and exploring the local ecosystem. The area is also inhabited by the indigenous Semelai people, who have a deep connection with the lake and its resources.

Historical Landmark

Seng Huat Temple Mengkuang

This is a Chinese temple located in the Mengkuang town of Bera district. The temple is known for its beautiful architecture and its location amidst gardens and a man-made cave. It's a popular spot for both religious pilgrims and tourists.

Hiking Trails & Wildlife Attractions

Mengkarak

Mengkarak

Difficulty: Moderate

Length: 9.2 km

Elevation gain: 178 m

Route type: Out & back

Mengkarak are known as a 9.2-km out-and-back trail near Bandar Bera, Pahanga which is a small village located in the mountains. Generally considered a moderately challenging route, it takes an average of 2 h 14 min to complete. The hike to get there involves some steep and slippery sections.

During the rainy season, the trail can become very muddy, so it is important to wear appropriate footwear. There are also a lot of mosquitoes in the area, so be sure to bring insect repellent.

This is a popular trail for birding, hiking, and mountain biking, but you can still enjoy some solitude during quieter times of day. The trail is open year-round and is beautiful to visit anytime.

Bukit Hulu Kekura

Bukit Hulu Kekura

Difficulty: Hard

Length: 19.3 km

Elevation gain: 645 m

Route type: Out & back

Discover this 19.3-km out-and-back trail near Bandar Bera, Pahang. Generally considered a challenging route, it takes an average of 5 h 39 min to complete. This trail is great for hiking, running, and walking, and it's unlikely you'll encounter many other people while exploring. The trail is open year-round and is beautiful to visit anytime.

This route winds through the green tropical rainforest, where you can expect to see many different kinds of animals and plants including monkeys, snakes, and birds. The route is mostly flat terrain, with some rocky sections. During the rainy season, some parts of the route may be muddy and slippery.
